1. Why a website is essential for health professionals

Imagine this situation: Ana, age 35, moves to Alicante and needs a dentist. What does she do? She doesn't ask neighbors she doesn't know yet for recommendations. She takes out her phone, searches "dentist in Alicante" and checks the first results. If your clinic doesn't appear, for Ana it simply doesn't exist.

In healthcare, trust is everything. And in 2026, the first impression doesn't happen in the consultation room, but on Google. According to a Doctoralia study, 82% of patients consult the internet before choosing a healthcare professional. They search for opinions, look at clinic photos, check the treatments offered and, above all, evaluate whether the professional gives them confidence.

A website for a dentist or physiotherapist is not a luxury: it's your digital business card. While an Instagram profile can show nice photos, only a professional website can provide all the information a patient needs to take the step of calling and requesting an appointment.

💡 Tip

If you think social media replaces a website, think again. Instagram and Facebook constantly change their algorithms, and you don't control who sees your content. Your website is yours: no one can take away your visibility or change the rules of the game.

A real case: Mediterranean Dental Clinic in Elche had only a social media profile for three years. When they created their professional website with detailed treatment information and appointment booking, first visit requests increased by 40% in two months. Why? Patients searching for "dentist Elche" on Google could finally find them directly.

2. Sections that must be on your dental clinic or physiotherapy website

Not all healthcare websites are the same, but there are sections that are non-negotiable if you want your physiotherapy or dental clinic website to truly generate appointments. Let's go through them one by one.

Home page that engages in 3 seconds

Your home page is the entrance. A patient decides in less than 3 seconds whether to stay or leave. You need: a clear headline explaining what you do and where ("Dental clinic in Valencia specializing in implants"), a real photo of the office or team, and a visible "Book Appointment" button. No endless sliders or generic text.

Complete list of treatments or services

Each treatment deserves its own section or page. For a dentist: orthodontics, implants, whitening, root canals, cleaning, pediatric dentistry. For a physiotherapist: sports physiotherapy, post-surgical rehabilitation, pelvic floor, dry needling, therapeutic massage. The patient wants to know if you offer exactly what they need before calling.

Information about the professional and team

People want to know who will be treating them. Include a professional photo, your training, your specializations, years of experience and registration number. A physiotherapist in Elda who shows their university degree, continued education courses and a smiling photo conveys much more confidence than an anonymous website.

⚠️ Important

Never use generic stock photos to represent your team or clinic. Patients immediately spot fake photos, and that destroys trust. Real photos, even if not perfect, always win.

Location, hours and contact

It seems obvious, but many healthcare websites hide this information. Your full address, an embedded Google Maps, consultation hours, clickable phone number (so patients can call with one tap on mobile) and a contact form or WhatsApp link. If you're in the center of Valencia or a well-known area of Alicante, mention it clearly.

Patient testimonials and reviews

90% of patients read opinions before choosing a healthcare professional. If you have good reviews on Google, integrate them into your website. If you have satisfied patients, ask them for a brief testimonial. A "What our patients say" section with 4-5 real opinions can be the final push for someone to book an appointment.

3. Web design for healthcare: how to inspire trust from the first click

Web design for health professionals has its own rules. It's not the same to design a restaurant website as it is to design a dental clinic website. Patients look for seriousness, visual cleanliness and professionalism. Here are the key principles.

Colors that convey health: Light blues, soft greens and whites are the kings of healthcare design. They convey cleanliness, calm and professionalism. Avoid loud colors, dark backgrounds or combinations that remind you more of a bar than a medical office. A physiotherapist in Valencia using light blue and white tones is already sending the right message before the patient reads a single word.

Readable typography: Use clean sans-serif fonts, minimum 16px for body text. Many of your patients may be older people browsing on mobile. If they have to squint to read, they leave.

Simple navigation: Maximum 5-6 options in the main menu. Home, Services/Treatments, About Us, Contact/Book Appointment, Blog (optional). Any patient should be able to find the information they're looking for in a maximum of 2 clicks.

💡 Tip

Include a "Book Appointment" or "Call Now" button visible on all pages, preferably in the fixed upper menu. Every page on your website is a possible entry point, and the patient should be able to contact you without having to search for how to do it.

Loading speed: A website that takes more than 3 seconds to load loses 53% of mobile visitors. Images should be optimized (WebP format, maximum 200KB), code clean and hosting fast. Don't load your website with unnecessary animations or heavy plugins.

Responsive mandatory: Over 70% of healthcare searches are done from mobile. If your website looks good on desktop but is a mess on mobile, you're losing 7 out of 10 potential patients. Responsive design is not optional: it's the foundation.

⚠️ Important

Healthcare websites that show indicative treatment prices generate 35% more appointment requests. Patients want to get an idea of costs before calling. You don't need to be exact, just ranges: "Dental cleaning from 40€" or "First physiotherapy consultation: 35-50€".

The million-euro question. We're going to be clear and transparent about website prices for dentists and physiotherapists, because there's a lot of confusion in the market.

Option 1: Custom website with traditional agency

A web design agency will charge you between 1,500€ and 5,000€ for a professional website for your clinic. Add hosting (100-200€/year), domain (15€/year), maintenance (200-500€/year) and content updates they'll charge separately. In total, your first year can easily cost 3,000-6,000€.

You can create a website yourself with platforms like WordPress or Wix. Approximate cost: domain (15€/year), hosting (60-120€/year) and a premium template (50-100€). Total: about 150-250€ the first year. The problem: you need time to learn, the result is usually amateur and if something breaks, you're on your own.
